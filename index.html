8 Types of Social Engineering

Involves sending emails or messages asking for private information or encouraging you to click an infected links

Different Types of Phishing

  • Email Phising:
    • Spam sent to many people at once.
  • Vishing (Voice phishing):
    • When a phisher calls or leaves a voicemail pretending to be from a trusted institution in hopes of gaining private information.
  • Smishing (SMS phishing):
    • Involves sending texts containing malicious links or probing for personal information.
  • URL Phising:
    • When a phisher sends a link to a fake website that looks legitimate to the victim. When clicking the link the victim is asked to provide private details that can be collected by the attacker.
  • In-session Phising:
    • Occurs when a victim is actively signed into an account or platform & a fake pop-up appears asking them to sign in again or to give sensitive information.

15 Tip To Protect Against Social Engineering

Don’t click links or open attachments that you aren’t expecting.

Protecting your privacy online is the first step to avoid social engineering attacks.

Be cautious if an online friend asks for a favour & personal information, but unwilling to verify their identity.

Remember the signs of social engineering & the different ways social engineering attacks can affect you.

If a promise, offer or a deal seems too good to be true, it probably is.

Be cautious if an online friend asks for a favour & personal information, but unwilling to verify their identity.

Create strong, unique passwords and change them periodically. Never use the same password twice, and don’t use common phrases or personal information.

Enabling 2FA on your online accounts can help keep your online data private & out of the hands of social engineers.

Enable email & text spam filters to catch potential phishing messages.

Don’t allow strangers to access your Wi-Fi network.

Use a VPN (virtual private network) when connecting to Wi-Fi.

If you notice unusual charges on your account, weird emails sent from your address or other strange activity, change your passwords immediately.

Keeping track of your devices helps you protect private data stored on your devices, such as account logins or having a keylogger installed without your knowledge.

Keep your software up to date to avoid security vulnerabilities that hackers love to exploit.

Using cybersecurity software can help protect you from falling victim to cyberattacks.
